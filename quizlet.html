Terms in this set (41)

Jamestown, VA
first permanent English settlement in mainland America, established in 1607 by the Virginia Company and named in honor of King James I.
House of Burgesses
the first elected legislative assembly in the New World established in the Colony of Virginia in 1619, representative colony set up by England to make laws and levy taxes but England could veto its legistlative acts.
Mayflower Compact (1620)
The first agreement for self-government in America. It was signed by the 41 men on the Mayflower and set up a government for the Plymouth colony.
Fundamental Orders of Connecticut
Written by Thomas Hooker, it is considered the first written constitution (plan of government) in British North America. Guaranteed individual rights and expanded voting rights.
Colonial Regions
New England, Middle, Southern
Mercantilism
An economic policy under which nations sought to increase their wealth and power by obtaining large amounts of gold and silver and by selling more goods than they bought
Triangular Trade
A three way system of trade during 1600-1800s Africa sent slaves to America, America sent Raw Materials(sugar) to Europe, and Europe sent Guns and Rum to Africa.
French and Indian War
a war in North America between France and Britain (both aided by indian tribes) over resources and territory in the great lakes region. England won.
Acts of Parliament
several acts were passed by British parliament (to pay off the debt from the F&I war)that upset the colonists including the stamp act and the tea tax.
Proclamation of 1763
law forbidding English colonists to settle west of the Appalachian Mountains
Stamp Act
1765; law that taxed printed goods, including: playing cards, documents, newspapers, etc. "no taxiation without representation"
Intorable Acts
Series of laws passed to punish MA after the Boston Tea Party, closed down Boston harbor; lead to the greater colonial unity at the FCC (first continental congress)
Battle of Lexington and Concord
1st battle of Revolutionary War; shot heard around the world; patriot victory at concord
Thomas Paine
American Revolutionary leader and pamphleteer (born in England) who supported the American colonist's fight for independence; wrote common sense
Declaration of Independence
1776 statement, issued by the Second Continental Congress, explaining why the colonies wanted independence from Britain. Authored by Thomas Jeff.
Battle of Saratoga
Turning point of the American Revolution. It was very important because it convinced the French to give the U.S. military support, and, most importantly, showed the French that the Americans had the potential to beat their enemy, Great Britain.
Valley Forge
Place where Washington's army spent the winter of 1777-1778, a 4th of troops died here from disease and malnutriton, Steuben comes and trains troops
Battle of Yorktown
1781 American victory in Virginia that forced the British to surrender
Treaty of Paris
agreement signed by British and American leaders that stated the United States of America was a free and independent country.
Articles of Confederation
1st Constitution of the U.S. (weaknesses-no executive, no judicial, no power to tax, no power to regulate trade)
Northwest Ordinance
a law passed by Congress in 1787 that specified how western lands would be governed; few success of AOC
Constitutuional Convention
held in Philadelphia in 1787 to create a new, stronger government for the nation
James Madison
"Father of the Constitution," due to the impact his ideas had on the us gov.
Great Compromise
Compromise made by Constitutional Convention in which states would have equal representation in one house of the legislature and representation based on population in the other house
Three-Fifths Compromise
Agreement that each slave counted as three-fifths of a person in determining representation in the House for representation and taxation purposes
Federalists
A term used to describe supporters of the Constitution during ratification debates in state legislatures.
Anti-Federalists
people who opposed the Constitution
Federalist Papers
Written by Hamilton, Jay, & Madison to support ratification of the U.S. Constituiton
Ratification Debate
Opponents (anti-federalists) feared central power and wanted Bill of Rights; Constitution ratified at conventions; ultimately ratified b/c support of Washington and Franklin (Federalists), Federalist Papers, promise to add Bill of Rights
John Locke
English philosopher who argued that people have natural rights
Montesquieu
separation of powers
Magna Carta, British Parliment, and English Bill of Rights
British infulences that affected US government
Federalism
A system in which power is divided between the national and state governments
Popular Sovereignty
Rule by the people
Repubicanism
people exercise their power by voting for political representatives
Amendment Process
2/3 vote in each house then ratified by 3/4 of the states
Bill of Rights
First 10 amendments to the Constitution
Due Process
protects natural rights from the government
Alexander Hamilton
Secretary of Treasury under Washington; reduced national debt;national bank; promoted industry
Tarrif
A tax on imported goods; supported in the north, not in south
Washington's Farewell Address
He warned against the dangers of political parties and foreign alliances.
